Research Philosophy

Good research starts before the first question is asked.

My Psychology and Criminology background taught me that behaviour is never random — it's shaped by environment, context, and systems people often can't articulate. That's the lens I bring to UX research: I'm less interested in what users say they do, and more interested in understanding why they do it.

I combine structured research methods with AI-assisted synthesis to move from raw insights to design decisions faster — without losing the human nuance that makes research worth doing. I'm especially drawn to products that serve communities whose behaviours and needs are underrepresented in mainstream design thinking.

Five years in customer service taught me something no certificate can: that the gap between what a product promises and what a user actually experiences is where the most important design work lives.
